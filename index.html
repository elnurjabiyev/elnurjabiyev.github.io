Key Finding

Positively charged PMACC brush active nanofiltration membranes achieve up to 120-fold Li/Mg selectivity at high magnesium feed ratios (75–95% Mg²⁺) via Donnan exclusion and differential dehydration energy — the highest reported for a brush active thin film composite membrane platform.

Innovation

First-ever covalently tethered crown ether brush membrane (BAM) via post-polymerization amide coupling of 4′-Aminobenzo-15-crown-5 onto PMAA chains, introducing size-selective Li⁺ coordination directly into the active layer.

Membrane Systems Compared

  • Positively charged PMACC (SI-ATRP)
  • Negatively charged PSVBS (SI-ATRP)
  • Zwitterionic PDMAPS brushes
  • Crown ether-incorporated PMAA brush active NF membranes

Significance

Addresses the critical Li/Mg bottleneck in brine processing for EV battery supply chains. DOE-funded (Award DE-SC0026294). Applicable to both high-Mg²⁺ pretreatment and post-pretreatment lithium production stages.

Key Finding

PVBMAC bottlebrush brush active nanofiltration membranes maintain ~80% CaCl₂ rejection at 200 mM — compared to near-zero for single-brush membranes — overcoming the charge-screening limitation at high ionic strength.

Innovation

First bottlebrush architecture in brush active thin film composite membranes: 50 kDa PHEMA backbone grafted by SI-ATRP, then BiBB-esterified to create dense secondary initiation sites for PVBMAC side-chain growth. XPS confirms uniform 4× charge enhancement.

Architecture Design

  • 50 kDa PHEMA backbone via SI-ATRP
  • BiBB esterification of PHEMA hydroxyl groups
  • PVBMAC side-chains grafted by SI-ATRP
  • PMACC vs PVBMAC comparison (swelling resolved)

Impact

Extends brush active nanofiltration membrane performance from dilute (2 mM) to industrially relevant (200 mM) salt concentrations. Opens path to brackish water desalination and selective lithium recovery at high salinity.

pH-Controlled SI-ATRP of Polyanion Brush Active Thin Film Composite Nanofiltration Membranes: Single-Brush and Bottlebrush Architectures for Enhanced Monovalent Anion Selectivity

Key Finding

First direct PSVBS brush grafting via pH-controlled SI-ATRP (pH 9.5 + HCl), eliminating toxic precursor monomers and post-polymerization deprotection. 25-fold Cl⁻/SO₄²⁻ selectivity — among the highest reported for polyanion brush active nanofiltration membranes.

pH Control Mechanism

HCl addition to pH 9.5 reduces electrostatic repulsion between incoming sulfonate monomers and the growing brush, while maintaining halide coordination to the Cu catalyst — preventing oxidative termination in aqueous ATRP.

Bottlebrush Achievement

  • ~90% Na₂SO₄ and ~80% NaCl rejection at 200 mM
  • 7-fold Cl/SO₄ selectivity at 200 mM — first report
  • 3.4× sulfonate density confirmed by XPS
  • Uniform surface coverage across all strips

Applications

Zero liquid discharge systems, chloride/sulfate separation in industrial wastewater, and selective anion recovery. Establishes pH-controlled SI-ATRP as a generalizable method for polyanion brush active thin film composite nanofiltration membranes.
