About Me

I am a PhD candidate and Research Scientist at Auburn University specializing in advanced polymer synthesis, membrane technology, and materials science. My research focuses on revolutionizing polyelectrolyte membrane technology through innovative bottom-up synthesis approaches.

I have developed breakthrough technologies including TEAM (Tethered Electrolyte Active-layer Membrane) and co-invented BARO (Brush Active Reverse Osmosis) membranes. My work on lithium-magnesium selective nanofiltration membranes achieved 100x selectivity improvement, addressing critical needs in the EV battery supply chain.

Research Areas

Advanced Membrane Technology

Revolutionary TEAM and BARO membrane technologies using surface-initiated polymerization for water desalination and ion separation.

Polymer Synthesis & Chemistry

Bottom-up synthesis of polymer brushes, polyelectrolytes, and crown ether-functionalized polymers for selective separations.

Lithium Extraction

Developing lithium-magnesium selective nanofiltration membranes with breakthrough 100x selectivity improvement for battery applications.
